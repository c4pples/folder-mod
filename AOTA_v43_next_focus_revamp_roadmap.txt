AOTA v43 Post-GER/FRA Nation Revamp Roadmap

Summary
- This roadmap defines the next focus-tree revamp wave after Germany and France, prioritizing distinct country identity over raw tree size.
- Italy is first priority due to Mediterranean system impact and major-power leverage.
- Korea + Manchuria are paired to intentionally separate sovereignty-survival gameplay from puppet-legitimacy gameplay.
- Czechoslovakia + Poland are paired to differentiate fortress-industry survival from encircled mobilization politics.
- Romania + Hungary + Bulgaria are grouped as a contrast-heavy Balkan/Danubian cluster centered on resource politics, revisionism, and compact opportunism.
- USA is explicitly deferred: no U.S. revamp work in this wave due to fragile splinter/civil-war architecture risk.

Method used
- Audit current bespoke focus coverage in this repository and existing major audit report.
- Determine where countries still rely on vanilla or non-bespoke logic.

Batch roadmap
Batch A: Italy
Why grouped this way
- Solo batch because Italy’s systemic footprint is large enough to require dedicated pacing and balance attention.

Unique design pillar
- “Prestige under strain”: regime stability vs fragmentation, service rivalry (army/navy/air), Mediterranean strategy, colonial overextension.

Overlap to avoid
- No Germany-lite expansion rail.
- No instant meme empire branch that bypasses stabilization phase.

Escalation risks to control
- Over-accelerated naval/colonial snowball before mid-game divergence.
- Excessive early war declarations from prestige branches.

Batch B: Korea + Manchuria
Why grouped this way
- Paired East Asia redesign ensures explicit differentiation of adjacent tags with shared pressures.

Korea pillar
- “Sovereignty under compression”: national assertion via modernization, intelligence, resistance, and survival diplomacy.

Manchuria pillar
- “Legitimacy in manufacture”: puppet-derived governance legitimacy, extraction economy choices, and autonomy bids.

Overlap to avoid
- Do not mirror political structures with renamed focuses.
- Korea != weaker Japan; Manchuria != conquest-only puppet client.

Escalation risks to control
- Prevent simultaneous runaway militarization in both tags.
- Gate late assertiveness behind internal consolidation milestones.

Batch C: Czechoslovakia + Poland
Why grouped this way
- Both are Central European pressure states but require opposite strategic feel.

Czechoslovakia pillar
- “Fortress industry under coalition strain”: internal national balancing, fortified defense economy, alliance dependence decisions.

Poland pillar
- “Encircled mobilizer”: officer corps/state cohesion tradeoffs, strategic balancing/federation options, stubborn survival doctrine.

Overlap to avoid
- CZE must not become generic democratic bunker.
- POL must not become generic revanchist rush path.

Escalation risks to control
- Avoid deterministic collapse scripts that erase player agency too early.
- Ensure defensive play can transition into consequential mid/late-game leverage.

Batch D: Romania + Hungary + Bulgaria
Why grouped this way
- Shared Danubian/Balkan theater with intentionally contrasting motivations.

Romania pillar
- “Oil and court politics”: resource diplomacy + monarchy/elite/army balancing.

Hungary pillar
- “Bounded revisionism”: restoration politics under patron and capacity constraints.

Bulgaria pillar
- “Compact spoiler”: nimble revanchist diplomacy with high vulnerability.

Overlap to avoid
- No template “revisionist branch copied three times.”
- Distinguish resource leverage (ROM), restoration ambition (HUN), and tactical opportunism (BUL).

Escalation risks to control
- Regional chain-reaction wars triggered too early by synchronized revision branches.
- Patron lock-in removing meaningful autonomous choices.

Batch E: Wider Balkan integration review
Why grouped this way
- Integration pass after batch D ensures cross-tag compatibility and crisis pacing.

Regional pillar
- “Interlocking pressures, distinct national logic.”

Overlap to avoid
- Avoid flattening Balkan tags into one diplomacy template.

Escalation risks to control
- Event/focus trigger collisions and contradictory claims timing.

Batch F: Smaller Western / Central minors review
Why grouped this way
- Final tuning wave after core flashpoints are stabilized.

Regional pillar
- “Support-role distinctiveness”: each minor gets a concise but coherent strategic identity.

Overlap to avoid
- No filler branches solely for focus count inflation.

Escalation risks to control
- Scope creep and content bloat without replay-value gains.

Country uniqueness matrix
Italy
1) Internal-political axis: regime stability vs fragmentation.
2) Strategic-geography axis: Mediterranean multi-front allocation.
3) Military identity: army/navy/air rivalry with asymmetric investment outcomes.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: prestige claims vs great-power insecurity.
5) Why not ideology reskin: core tension is status management under structural overextension, not ideology swap.

Korea
1) Internal-political axis: sovereignty consolidation under external pressure.
2) Strategic-geography axis: buffer survival between larger powers.
3) Military identity: intelligence/resistance-centric national defense before full conventional parity.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: align, hedge, or resist under coercive neighborhood dynamics.
5) Why not ideology reskin: gameplay is survival-modernization assertion, not imported bloc politics.

Manchuria
1) Internal-political axis: legitimacy crisis of manufactured statehood.
2) Strategic-geography axis: exposed continental hinge tied to neighboring great-power interests.
3) Military identity: security apparatus and autonomy-capable force transition.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: patron compliance vs gradual sovereign assertion.
5) Why not ideology reskin: identity is about state authenticity and economic control, not generic ideology ladders.

Czechoslovakia
1) Internal-political axis: multinational balancing and cohesion.
2) Strategic-geography axis: defensible industrial bastion in Central Europe.
3) Military identity: fortress-industrial defensive doctrine.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: alliance dependency vs self-reliant deterrence.
5) Why not ideology reskin: distinctiveness comes from engineered resilience and coalition strain, not ideology labels.

Poland
1) Internal-political axis: officer corps cohesion vs broader state integration.
2) Strategic-geography axis: strategic encirclement management.
3) Military identity: mobilization-heavy survival doctrine with selective counterstroke windows.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: federation/balancing ambitions vs immediate survival constraints.
5) Why not ideology reskin: path identity is defined by exposure and consequence, not doctrine color.

Romania
1) Internal-political axis: monarchy/elite/army bargaining equilibrium.
2) Strategic-geography axis: border pressure around resource-rich core.
3) Military identity: resource-protection and flexible regional intervention posture.
4) Foreign-policy dilemma: oil diplomacy between competing patrons.

Hungary
1) Internal-political axis: restoration-elite legitimacy contest.
2) Strategic-geography axis: constrained interior position with revision targets.
3) Military identity: constrained rebuilding with selective modernization.

5) Why not ideology reskin: constrained ambition and legitimacy politics define play loop.

Bulgaria
1) Internal-political axis: revanchist pressure vs regime survivability.
2) Strategic-geography axis: compact, vulnerable Balkan crossroads.
3) Military identity: limited-force opportunism and timing-focused campaigns.

Major design risks
- Over-template risk: copying branch skeletons across countries with renamed text.
- Escalation pacing risk: too many early offensive unlocks compressing strategic arc.
- Dependency risk: Balkan and Central Europe claim/event timing collisions.
- Identity dilution risk: ideology symmetry overwhelming national internal logic.
- Scope risk: turning tuning candidates into full rebuilds without justified uniqueness gains.
- Technical risk: interaction with existing bespoke majors if diplomacy/event hooks are not staged.
- Explicit deferred risk: U.S. revamp excluded due to fragile splinter/civil-war structure.

Recommended implementation sequence (post-GER/FRA)
1) Batch A (Italy) — full rebuild + validation pass.
2) Batch B (Korea + Manchuria) — paired differentiation sprint.
3) Batch C (Czechoslovakia + Poland) — Central Europe contrast pass.
4) Batch D (Romania + Hungary + Bulgaria) — Danubian/Balkan contrast pass.
5) Batch E (Wider Balkan integration review) — trigger/timing harmonization.
6) Batch F (Smaller Western/Central minors review) — targeted tuning and selective redesign.

Execution guardrails for all batches
- Distinctness over size: reject branches that do not add a country-specific gameplay identity.
- No map/province/state rewrites in this roadmap wave.
- No filler branch inflation.
- Keep early stabilization -> mid divergence -> late consolidation pacing.
- Log risky dependencies before implementation starts in each batch memo.

Deferred / lower-priority countries
- USA: explicitly deferred (high-risk splinter/civil-war structure).
- Non-priority peripheral tags not in Tier 1/2: hold for post-Batch F evaluation unless escalation tests identify a blocking gap.

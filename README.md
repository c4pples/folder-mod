# Ashes of the Armistice

*The Great War ended in a Central Powers non-defeat, but peace never truly began.*

**Ashes of the Armistice (AOTA)** is a Hearts of Iron IV alternate-history mod set in a world defined by a drawn armistice, exhausted empires, and political systems pushed past their design limits. Across Europe, the Americas, and beyond, governments are no longer asking how to return to normal — they are asking what kind of order can survive the century that follows.

## Overview

AOTA is built around one central premise: the postwar settlement is unstable, temporary, and broadly mistrusted. The war ended in a Central Powers-shaped negotiated armistice rather than decisive civilizational victory, leaving every major state with unresolved grievances, brittle institutions, and competing visions of legitimacy.

This produces a world where constitutional governments, military directorates, monarchies, socialist coalitions, anarchist movements, and market-radical regimes all contend to define the "second peace" on their own terms.

## World Situation

By game start, the international order still exists on paper — treaties, conferences, and ministries remain — but mounting crisis has outpaced the institutions meant to contain it.

- **Germany** faces the Rhine question and the politics of revision under pressure.
- **France** is pulled between republican continuity, social upheaval, and revolutionary currents.
- **Britain** still commands an empire, but with visible overstretch and deep imperial strain.
- **Austria-Hungary** survives, yet its internal compromise is increasingly unstable.
- **Serbia** functions as a constrained Danubian client state rather than a fully free regional pole.
- **Hungarian and Yugoslav projects** remain politically active but lack fully sovereign settlement closure at game start.
- **Ukraine** is an active eastern borderland actor, independent under the Kyiv Provisional Sovereignty settlement but constantly contested and not a starting puppet of Berlin or Moscow.
- **Poland** exists through the Warsaw Guarantees compromise: independent, but treaty-constrained inside an unresolved corridor order.
- **Russia** remains politically unfinished, with reunification as an open strategic project.
- **The Ottoman Empire** endures as an imperial shell under severe provincial and structural stress.
- **The United States** begins in deadlock and can fracture into multiple successor regimes.

The result is a setting of managed decline, hard bargaining, and accelerating confrontation rather than immediate total war.

## Major Powers & Geopolitical Fault Lines

### The European Settlement Crisis
AOTA repeatedly returns to unresolved frontier and treaty questions — especially around the Rhine, Danube, and wider Central/Eastern Europe — as the core engine of escalation.

### Imperial Strain and Colonial Burden
Britain, France, and other imperial actors are depicted as formally intact but materially overstretched, with colonial commitments and garrison pressures shaping strategic choices.

### Russia's Reunification Dilemma
Russia is framed less as a settled great power and more as a contested state project: centralization, regional bargaining, ideology, and military reconstruction all compete to define what "Russia" becomes.

### The American Fracture
AOTA's U.S. content centers on collapse and succession politics: constitutional remnant rule, worker revolution, nationalist militarization, Pacific federalism, corporate governance, agrarian regionalism, and Christian communitarian alternatives all appear as playable trajectories.

### The New Late-Game Blocs
Current content also introduces a hard-edged global bloc struggle between coercive "order" and reconstruction-oriented alternatives, including the **Night Pact** and the **Charter of Restoration** framework.

## Gameplay Identity

AOTA emphasizes:

- **Political identity over generic expansion:** campaigns are driven by regime character, legitimacy crises, and ideological conflict, not just map-painting tempo.
- **Narrative escalation:** event prose and national spirits frame war as the outcome of unresolved settlements and state breakdown.
- **Divergent state models:** constitutional, authoritarian, socialist, anarchist, monarchist, and market-radical routes are treated as materially distinct governance projects.
- **Crisis management as strategy:** stability, reconstruction, coercion, and coalition design matter as much as battlefield execution.

## Current Content Highlights

The repository currently includes broad country coverage and multiple major overhauls, with especially visible work in:

- **Germany, France, Britain, Russia, the Ottoman Empire, and the United States** as major setting anchors.
- **American splinter-state gameplay** (ACC, AWU, ANA, APF, ACG, ASL, CCW), including reunification framing and branch-specific flavor.
- **Imperial and colonial pressure content** for several dominions/colonial actors.
- **Expanded worldbuilding ideas/events** that deepen faction identity and global crisis tone.
- **Formable/flagship and thematic event chains**, including later-phase bloc dynamics.

AOTA also contains a wide set of bespoke national focus files for majors and minors, with scope and depth varying by country.

## Design Philosophy & Tone

AOTA's writing and structure present a serious interwar world where legitimacy is scarce, memory of war is politically active, and "peace" is often administrative rather than moral. The mod's tone favors strategic tragedy, institutional stress, and ideological struggle over romanticized triumph.

In practice, this means campaigns are often about what kind of state survives crisis — and what that survival costs.

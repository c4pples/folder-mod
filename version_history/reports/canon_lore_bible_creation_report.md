## B) Major themes identified

1. Unfinished peace and deferred settlement logic as world core.
2. Administrative continuity masking legitimacy breakdown.
3. Militarized politics and emergency governance as normalized tools.
4. Imperial overstretch and colonial coercion-resource contradiction.
5. Fragmentation/reunification meta-conflicts (Russia, China, USA).
6. Broad ideological plurality beyond standard HOI4 binaries.
7. Elite coalition politics (cartels, staffs, committees, provincial blocs) as central driver.
8. Strategic tragedy with disciplined, conditional hope pathways.

---

## C) Major ambiguities preserved intentionally

1. Exact detailed chronology of divergence from OTL prior to game start is implied more than fully narrated.
2. Several focus descriptions remain generic/template-like, requiring inference from names, event prose, and spirit logic rather than explicit narrative blocks.
3. Extent of Germany’s and other powers’ pre-1936 extra-European influence remains conservative by design in current canon files.
4. Some leader portrait/desc assets are placeholders or reused; political role is clearer than biographical depth in certain tags.
5. China’s high-level legitimacy/reunification framework is clear, while some factional granularities remain open for future passes.

These ambiguities were retained in the lore bible where they appear to be part of mod development stage or deliberate atmospheric openness.

## D) Regions/countries with strongest lore support

## Very strong support
- Germany, France, Britain, Russia, Ottoman Empire, United States, Italy.
- Major crisis systems: Rhine, Danube, Russian reunification pressure, Ottoman provincial pressure, U.S. constitutional breakdown.
- Late arc worldbuilding: Night Pact vs Charter of Restoration.

## Strong support
- Japan/China strategic framework (fragmentation vs reunification and response logic).
- Colonial stress regions (Algeria, Congo, Raj).
- U.S. splinter successor identities and ideological differentiation.

## Moderate support
- Ireland, Finland, Ukraine (new institutional flavor + actor clusters, but narrower depth than majors).
- Belgium/Poland/Serbia/Spain thematic arcs visible through event prose and idea naming.

## E) Regions/countries needing future expansion

1. Latin America outside U.S./Cuba late-arc hooks.
2. Wider African political societies beyond Algeria/Congo snapshots.
3. Deeper Middle East provincial actor mapping within Ottoman sphere.
4. East Asian social-class and institutional writing depth beyond core claimant mechanics.
5. Minor European states with existing tree presence but thinner prose identity.

Recommendation: future passes should expand these areas using the same institution-first, actor-driven narrative style already established for major powers and enhanced minors.

---

## F) How future Codex passes should use the lore bible

1. Use `canon_game_start_lore_bible.md` as baseline before rewriting major content.
2. Maintain setting tone (serious, political, institution-aware, anti-boilerplate).
3. Preserve country-specific political language and social structure logic.
4. Write leaders/advisors/generals as active historical agents with constituencies and methods.
5. If extending canon, document extension explicitly and keep it conservative.
6. If revising canon, declare a dedicated canon update pass rather than silently drifting baseline.

---

## G) Outcome

The new lore bible consolidates dispersed worldbuilding into one reusable internal canon reference focused on game-start reality, preserves intentional ambiguity, and establishes a consistent framework for future writing, balancing, and content expansion.

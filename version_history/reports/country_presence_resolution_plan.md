# Country Presence Resolution Plan

_Designed from `country_presence_and_content_coverage_audit` findings, with lore cross-check against `version_history/lore/canon_game_start_lore_bible.md` and current content footprint in `common/` + `events/` files._

## A. Executive summary

- Undercovered present-at-start countries reviewed: **51**.
- Recommended **direct absorb/annex outcomes**: **2** (`AFA`, `TAN`).
- Recommended **subordinate/client outcomes**: **11** (`BHU`, `BRM`, `ICE`, `IRQ`, `KUW`, `NEP`, `OMA`, `PHI`, `SER`, `SYR`, `YEM`).
- Recommended **remain independent with real content expansion**: **19** (`ALG`, `BEL`, `BUL`, `COG`, `EGY`, `ETH`, `GXC`, `MAN`, `MEN`, `PER`, `POR`, `SAU`, `SHX`, `SIA`, `SIK`, `SPR`, `XSM`, `YUN`, `VEN`).
- Recommended **remain independent but tightly passive (explicitly constrained behavior package)**: **18** (`BOL`, `CHL`, `COL`, `COS`, `DOM`, `ECU`, `ELS`, `GUA`, `HAI`, `HON`, `LIB`, `MON`, `NIC`, `PAN`, `PAR`, `PRU`, `URG`, `TIB`).
- **Manual review (high lore sensitivity / branching dependency)**: **1** (`AFG` alternate path if Persia-India arc is expanded in next cycle).

### Biggest restructuring opportunities
1. **Ottoman-Arab provincial restructuring**: convert currently unsupported Arab tags into a coherent imperial-periphery system (autonomous vilayets/protectorates, not random sovereign AI actors).
2. **China frontier ring consolidation**: turn all warlord-edge tags into one coordinated “Frontier Realignment” system rather than tag-by-tag ad hoc logic.
3. **Americas anti-chaos guardrails**: formalize a hemispheric low-war framework so unsupported minors remain geopolitical texture without AI war spam.

### Biggest lore-sensitive cases
- **SER** must remain a constrained client under Danubian/Ottoman-era balance assumptions, not a free factional great-power aspirant.
- **SYR/IRQ/KUW/YEM/OMA** should align with the surviving Ottoman legal-security architecture in the lore bible.
- **AFG/PER/SAU** are corridor-defining states and cannot be left both empty and fully free.

## C. Annexation / absorption recommendations

### 1) Direct absorption

- **ETH absorbs AFA** at game start.
  - Result: Horn map simplification without losing regional identity.
  - Add releasable Afar path via revolt decision if Ethiopia collapses.
- **RUS-aligned authority absorbs TAN** at game start.
  - Result: cleaner Siberian front with fewer unsupported edge behaviors.
  - Add releasable Tannu insurgent tag tied to Russian instability.

### 2) Subordinate/client architecture (preferred over annexation)

#### Ottoman-Arab provincial bloc
- `IRQ`, `SYR` as **autonomous vilayets/client administrations**.
- `KUW`, `OMA`, `YEM` as **protectorate/concessionary maritime dependencies**.
- Why: preserves lore-defined Ottoman legal continuity while giving differentiated local politics.

#### South Asia dependency ring
- `BHU`, `NEP` as **protected mountain dependencies** tied to `RAJ` security logic.
- `BRM` as **administrative dependency with unrest mechanics** (not free sovereign power).

#### North Atlantic / Pacific structured dependencies
- `ICE` as **Danish home-rule dependency**.
- `PHI` as **US-linked commonwealth dependency** with staged autonomy.

#### Danubian-Balkan client formalization
- `SER` retained on-map but reclassified as **Austro-Danubian client** with constrained military diplomacy.

## D. Content-expansion recommendations (countries that remain active)

## Tier 1 (full mini-arc, 25–40 focuses + bespoke systems)
- `EGY`, `PER`, `SPR`, `SIA`, `VEN`.
- Required package:
  - Distinct state identity branch (regime legitimacy model).
  - 2–3 national spirits with upgrades.
  - 8–12 events anchored in regional crises.
  - 1 decision system (canal/oil/court-military/neutrality balancing).
  - 6–10 characters (leaders, ministers, military).
  - Explicit AI doctrine (defensive/regional, no global faction-leading unless scripted).

## Tier 2 (targeted strengthening, 12–24 focuses)
- `ALG`, `BEL`, `BUL`, `COG`, `ETH`, `POR`, `SAU`.
- Required package:
  - One coherent domestic arc.
  - One regional diplomacy lane.
  - Defensive military identity and industrial profile.
  - “Should not do” constraints codified (no early major wars, no faction leadership).

## Tier 3 (China frontier cluster expansion)
- `GXC`, `MAN`, `MEN`, `SHX`, `SIK`, `XSM`, `YUN`.
- Required package:
  - Shared “Frontier Realignment” framework.
  - Legitimacy/resource/militia variables common to all seven tags.
  - Scripted interaction with `CHI` and `JAP` so they serve pacing rather than derailment.
  - Unified AI pacing plan: local wars possible, continental snowball prevention enforced.

## Passive countries content floor (must still be “real content”, not blank)
- Tags: `BOL`, `CHL`, `COL`, `COS`, `DOM`, `ECU`, `ELS`, `GUA`, `HAI`, `HON`, `LIB`, `MON`, `NIC`, `PAN`, `PAR`, `PRU`, `URG`, `TIB`.
- Minimum package:
  - 1 persistent spirit (“Limited Strategic Bandwidth”).
  - 2–4 flavor events.
  - AI blocklist for faction leadership and major offensive wars.
  - Regional non-aggression/neutrality decision hooks.

### What these countries should NOT do (converted into active constraints)
- No unsupported tag may **lead a faction** without passing a high-threshold scripted gate.
- No unsupported/light tag may receive **large industrial snowball buffs** before 1941.
- No unsupported tag may open **major offensive wars** without specific scripted crisis triggers.
- No unsupported tag may perform **rapid ideology wildcard flips** without internal instability penalties.

## E. Regional strategy summary

## Europe (Low Countries, Balkans, Iberia)
- Expand `BEL`, `BUL`, `POR`, `SPR`; formalize `SER` as client.
- Goal: preserve diplomatic texture while preventing low-content Balkan/Iberian chaos branches.

## Balkans / Danubian fringe
- `SER` should remain politically alive but sovereignty-constrained.
- Add conference/event structure tying Serbia to Danubian instability rather than free conquest.

## Middle East
- Build Ottoman provincial hierarchy (`IRQ`, `SYR`, `KUW`, `OMA`, `YEM` as clients).
- Expand `EGY`, `PER`, `SAU` as the three active poles in the region.

## Africa
- Expand `ALG`, `COG`, `ETH`; absorb `AFA`; keep `LIB` passive neutral.
- Preserve colonial coercion vs autonomy tensions as core gameplay driver.

## Asia
- China warlord cluster gets unified expansion framework.
- South Asia frontier (`BHU`, `NEP`, `BRM`) becomes dependency ring rather than unsupported sovereign trio.
- `TIB` and `MON` stay passive buffers; `TAN` absorbed.

## Americas
- Keep most Central America + lower-priority Andean/Caribbean tags passive with hard constraints.
- Promote `VEN` as the only undercovered Americas tag into active expansion tier due to oil/geostrategic leverage.

## Implementation sequencing recommendation (roadmap cadence)

- **Wave 1 (stability patch):** all subordination/absorption changes + passive AI constraints.
- **Wave 2 (regional anchors):** Tier 1 expansion countries.
- **Wave 3 (cluster systems):** China frontier cluster unified framework.
- **Wave 4 (polish):** Tier 2 expansions + flavor pass for passive states.

This sequencing ensures no unsupported active tag remains in “awkward middle state” after Wave 1, while preserving room for deeper content where lore and gameplay justify independence.

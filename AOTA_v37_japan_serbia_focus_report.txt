Nation implementation memos:

JAPAN (Major Scope)
- Design intent: high-agency major with early stabilization, ideological divergence in mid-game, and late strategic doctrine/foreign-policy consolidation.
- Branch plan:
  1) Armistice stabilization spine
  2) Political direction split (Imperial-authoritarian, constitutional cabinet, workers' reconstruction)
  3) Industrial modernization (ports, arsenals, education, fuel and procurement)
  4) Military modernization (army/navy/air + joint doctrine)
  5) Foreign policy posture (continental, southern commercial, balancing/neutral conference)
- Implemented focus count: 72
- Major rewards profile: moderate PP/stability cadence, controlled factory injections, selective doctrine/tech boosts, no front-loaded snowball wargoal spam.
- Risks: high branch count increases AI path variability; monitor late-branch convergence tuning in playtests.

SERBIA (Standard Minor Scope)
- Design intent: deep but constrained regional tree focused on regime settlement, industrial survivability, and defensive military readiness before wider Balkan diplomacy.
- Branch plan:
  1) Post-breakup stabilization spine
  2) Political split (royalist emergency order, civic republicanism, workers' federal path)
  3) Economic survivability and infrastructure repair
  4) Military defensive modernization
  5) Regional diplomacy and second-reckoning preparation
- Implemented focus count: 65
- Major rewards profile: measured manpower and industry gains, defense-first military progression, restrained diplomatic payoff.
- Risks: shared Yugoslav strategic context means balance should be observed versus neighboring bespoke trees after AI simulations.
